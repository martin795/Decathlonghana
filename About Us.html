History

Founded by Michel Leclercq in 1976, Decathlon started with a store in Lille, France. Its holding company was formerly known as Oxylane. The company expanded abroad to Germany in 1986, Spain in 1992, Italy in 1993, Belgium in 1997, Portugal, the United KIngdom in 1999, Brazil in 2001, mainland China in 2003, India and Romania in 2009, Turkey and Czech Republic in 2010, Taiwan and Lebanon in 2012, Hong Kong in 2013, Malaysia, Singapore, and Mexico in 2016, South Arica, Phillippines, Tunisia and Indonesia in 2017;in South Korea, and Austrailia in 2018, and Canada in 2019. In 2025, Michel Abellea was appointed as CEO of Decatylon. In September 2019, Decathlon reached an agreement with Alltricks, an online retailer of accessories for cyclists and runners. In January 2022, Barbara Martin Coppola was appoitrd CEO. In 2022 Decathlon closed their retail store presenece in United States. In 2023, Decathlon opened a training center in Lille. By October 2023, Decathlon had nine training centers in partnership with Afpa Entreprises, an association for the professional training of adults, and three centers of its own on its presmises. In November 2023, Decathlon acquired Bergfreunde, an online specialist retailer for mountain sports, climbing, and outdoor equipment. Decathlon isnincreasing its investments to boost production and expand its retail presence in India, from where it presently exports around 65% of its output to international markets, according to Global CEO Barbara Martin Coppola. In, July 2024, Decathlon reported its investment Recyc'Elit, a start-up specialized in the chemical recycling of polyster textiles and complex PET plastics.
